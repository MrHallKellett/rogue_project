Programming Project 9

Rogue

Rogue is a pioneering and influential video game that was first released in 1980. It is considered one of the earliest and most significant examples of the "roguelike" genre, a type of role-playing game characterized by procedurally generated levels, permadeath (the player's character dying permanently), and turn-based gameplay.

In Rogue, the player takes on the role of a character who must descend into an elaborate dungeon, battling monsters, collecting treasure and powerful items, and ultimately attempting to retrieve the Amulet of Yendor from the deepest levels. The game features a top-down, ASCII-based visual style, where the dungeon, characters, and items are represented by various keyboard characters.

Your task is to create a roguelike game, evidencing your programming skills up to IGCSE standard (and beyond, if you like).

  1. Use the record definition above to create a variable named Player. This variable will store the x and y coordinates of the player during the game.

  1. Define a function named MovePlayer() that accepts Player and Direction as parameters.

You will need to consider how 0-based indexing affects your data validation routines used here.

  1. If the new x and y position are OK, display a message telling us where the new player will be.

Notice because we are returning a Coordinates record, we will see the memory address of the variable instead of its value. Don't worry about this at this stage!
